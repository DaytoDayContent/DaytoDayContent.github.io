Big Idea

Tung Tung Sahur is a viral AI-generated meme character tied to the Indonesian/Malay sahur wake-up tradition, later absorbed into the broader “Italian brainrot” meme universe.

Most Testable

Know the meaning of “sahur,” the role of the kentongan/drumming sound, the February 2025 TikTok origin, and how remix culture helped it spread.

Common Mistake

Do not treat it as purely Italian. It became linked to Italian brainrot, but its phrase and cultural reference are Indonesian/Malay Ramadan-related.

Study tip: Use the timeline first, then quiz yourself. The goal is to understand the chain: cultural sound → AI character → TikTok meme → remix/fan lore.

Concept Sheet

Core term

Sahur

The pre-dawn meal eaten by Muslims before fasting during Ramadan. The meme's phrase references waking people for this meal.

Sound

“Tung Tung Tung”

An onomatopoeic imitation of rhythmic knocking/drumming used in wake-up calls. In meme form, the sound becomes the character's identity.

Object

Kentongan

A traditional wooden slit drum or signal instrument associated with community alerts. Meme explainers connect the character to this wooden instrument.

Platform

TikTok Origin

The meme is widely traced to TikTok in February 2025, often credited to user @noxaasht in meme databases and explainers.

Genre

AI Brainrot

A style of absurd, AI-generated characters, dramatic narration, surreal visuals, and chaotic fan-made lore.

Spread

Remix Culture

The meme spread through edits, songs, game references, short-form video reposts, and fan-made storylines with other brainrot characters.

Timeline View

Before the meme: Ramadan wake-up tradition

In some communities, rhythmic knocking or drumming is used to wake people for sahur before dawn.

February 2025: AI TikTok meme appears

The meme becomes associated with an AI-generated wooden/anthropomorphic figure and an Indonesian voice-over.

2025: Short-form remix spread

TikTok, edits, music remixes, and reposts turn the character into a recognizable brainrot figure.

Afterward: absorbed into “Italian brainrot” lore

Even though the phrase has Indonesian/Malay Ramadan roots, online communities place the character next to other absurd AI brainrot characters.
